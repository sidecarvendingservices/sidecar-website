How Much Does A Vending Machine Cost In Atlanta?

It depends which question you're actually asking — and most people asking it don't realize there are two.

By Brian Morris, co-founder of Sidecar Vending Services · July 2026

We get some version of this question a lot, and it usually splits into two very different things: "what would it cost me to buy and run a vending machine myself?" and "what would it cost my business to have a vending machine installed?" Here's the honest answer to both.

If you're buying a machine yourself

Industry pricing guides commonly cite a wide range depending on the type of machine:

  • Used, standard snack or drink machines: roughly $1,200–$3,000.
  • New standard machines: roughly $3,000–$6,000 depending on snack, drink, or combo configuration.
  • Smart or AI-powered machines with touchscreens and remote monitoring: anywhere from $3,000 up to $15,000 or more for premium systems.

That's just the equipment. Ongoing costs — restocking, electricity, card-processing fees, maintenance, and any commission paid to the host location — typically add up to somewhere in the neighborhood of $50–$150 per machine per month, on top of the time it takes to actually manage all of it.

The real cost isn't always the dollars

A well-placed machine can gross a few hundred dollars a month and take roughly a year to pay off the equipment — which can work out fine if running vending machines is the business you're actually in. For most offices, warehouses, apartment communities, gyms, medical facilities, and dealerships, it isn't. The bigger cost is attention: sourcing product, keeping it stocked, fixing what breaks, and fielding complaints when it isn't — all for a break room amenity that isn't the point of the business.

What it costs to have Sidecar install one instead

This is the second question, and for a Sidecar location the answer is simpler: nothing. We cover the machine, installation, stocking, and ongoing maintenance entirely — there's no equipment purchase, no monthly fee, and no capital outlay on your end. Our revenue comes from the purchases customers make at the machine, the same way any vending operator makes money, which is why there's no separate charge to the business hosting it.
