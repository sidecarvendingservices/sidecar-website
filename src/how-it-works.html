Three Steps. Then You Forget About Us.

Getting a free vending machine installed is a three-step process: we evaluate your space, we install everything at no cost, and then we keep it stocked and running so you never have to think about it again.

1

We come to you — free on-site evaluation

Schedule a free evaluation and a Sidecar representative visits your space in person. We look at foot traffic, available space, and what your team, residents, members, or customers are likely to want, then recommend the right machine size and starting product mix. This visit costs nothing and comes with no obligation to move forward.

2

We handle setup — installed at no cost to you

Once you're ready, we install the machine at no cost to your business — no capital outlay and no logistics for your team to manage, just a simple service agreement to get started. That agreement is our promise to keep the machine stocked and maintained, and it comes with direct contact information for a dedicated representative you can reach anytime — we only ask for 30 days' notice if you ever need the machine relocated. We handle delivery, placement, and getting the machine online and accepting payments before we leave.

3

We stay on it — restocking, repairs, and monitoring

Smart inventory monitoring tells us when a machine is running low before it goes empty, so restocking happens on a real schedule instead of a guess. If anything breaks, a local Metro Atlanta technician responds the same day — not a national call center. Restocks, repairs, and upgrades are all included going forward.

Common Questions

The process, answered.

How long does the whole process take, from first contact to an installed machine?

After you request your free evaluation, a member of the Sidecar team contacts you within 24 hours to schedule the on-site visit. Installation timing after that depends on machine availability and your space, and we'll give you a specific timeline once we've seen the space.

Do we have to sign anything to get started?

Yes — and it works in your favor. The service agreement is our written promise to keep your machine stocked, maintained, and running, and it comes with direct contact information for a dedicated representative who can help with anything, any time. In return, we simply ask for 30 days' notice if you ever need a machine relocated. There's no capital outlay on your end — the agreement just formalizes our commitment to quality service.

What does "smart inventory monitoring" actually mean?

Our machines track what's selling and what's running low, so restocking visits are based on real usage instead of a fixed schedule — meaning popular items get replenished faster and slower-moving items don't go to waste.

What if we want to change the product mix later?

That's expected and easy — we adjust the mix over time based on what's actually selling and any feedback you pass along.
